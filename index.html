How to Get and Upload Your Zip File

These steps will guide you through obtaining your Instagram data in a zip file and uploading it here. This process is designed to be easy and anonymous—only you will have access to your data. If you change your mind at any point, don’t worry—you don’t have to undo anything.

  1. Go to your Instagram profile page and open Settings (☰ menu in the top right).
  2. Tap "Accounts Center".
  3. Scroll down and select "Your information and permissions".
  4. Tap "Download your information", then select "Download or transfer information".
  5. Choose "Some of your information".
  6. Scroll down, select "Followers and following", and tap "Next".
  7. Select "Download to device".
  8. Tap "Date range", choose "All time", and press "Save".
  9. Tap "Create files"—you’ll receive an email from Instagram when your data is ready.

How to Download and Upload Your Zip File

Once Instagram gets your zip file ready, follow these steps on an iPhone to download your IG zip file and upload it here. If you don't have an iPhone or they don't line up perfectly, the steps should be very similar and easy to match on your device.

  1. Either wait a few minutes (about 10 minutes at most) or wait for the email from Instagram confirming your zip file is ready.
  2. Navigate back to "Download your information" (as you did in step 4 above) and click "Download" on your file (typically starts with "01/01/04" and ends with "specific information download").
  3. Instagram will ask you to re-enter your password (on their site, not on instaghosts.com), then click "Continue".
  4. This will open a new tab in your browser. Download your zip file to Files (or any preferred location—this doesn’t necessarily matter). To do this, click "Save...", select Files, then click "Save" and close the tab.
  5. Go back to instaghosts.com, click "Choose File", then select your zip file to upload.
  6. In case your zip file isn't clearly visible, go to "On My iPhone", your browser name (like "Chrome"), and click the zip file with the current date. It should start with "instagram-"
